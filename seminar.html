10/22 - Peter McGrath (NCSU)

Title: Extremal Domains for the First Eigenvalue of the Laplacian.
Abstract: The Faber-Krahn inequality asserts that the round balls uniquely minimize the first eigenvalue of the Laplacian among domains in \(\mathbb{R}^n\) with the same volume. In 1970, Serrin proved more generally that balls are the only critical points for the functional assigning a domain in \(\mathbb{R}^n\) to its first eigenvalue. Much less is known about the analogous problem for domains in the round \(n\)-sphere, and for \(n = 2\), Souam conjectured (2005) the only critical domains are rotationally symmetric disks and annuli. I will discuss the construction of counterexamples (joint work with C. Hines and J. Kolesar) to this conjecture.
